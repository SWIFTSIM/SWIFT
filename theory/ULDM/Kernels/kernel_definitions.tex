
\subsection{Kernel}

Lets define the kernel function as:

\begin{equation}
W(\vec{x},h) = \frac{1}{h^n} f\left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right),
\end{equation}

where $n$ is the dimentionallity of the problem, 1, 2 or 3 and
$f(u)$ is a dimensionless function.

\subsection{Kernel Derivatives}

Using:
\begin{eqnarray}
  \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right) & = & \frac{1}{h}\frac{x}{|\vec{x}|} \\
  \nabla\left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right) & = & \frac{1}{h}\frac{\vec{x}}{|\vec{x}|} \\
   \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left( \frac{1}{|\vec{x}|} \right) & = & -\frac{x}{|\vec{x}|^3} 
\end{eqnarray}
on can compute kernel derivatives.

\noindent

The first derivative with respect to $x_i$ of the kernel function is:

\begin{align}
\frac{\partial}{\partial x_i} W(\vec{x},h) &= \frac{1}{h^{n+1}}f'\left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right) \frac{x_i}{|\vec{x}|}.
\end{align}
Note that for all the kernels listed above, $f'(0) = 0$. 

One can deduce the gradient of the kernel:

\begin{align}
 \vec\nabla_x W(\vec{x},h) &= \frac{1}{h^{n+1}}f'\left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right) \frac{\vec{x}}{|\vec{x}|}.
\end{align}

\noindent
In order to compute the quantum potential, the Laplacian of the kernel is needed. It writes:

\begin{align}
\frac{\partial^2 W(\vec{x},h)}{\partial x_i^2}
= \frac{1}{h^{n+1}} \left[
\frac{x_i^2}{h |\vec{x}|^2}\, f''\!\left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right) +
\frac{|\vec{x}|^2 - x_i^2}{|\vec{x}|^3}\,f'\!\left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right)\right]
\end{align}

\noindent
The Laplacian writes:


\begin{align}
\nabla^2 W(\vec{x},h)
= \frac{1}{h^{n+2}}
\left[
f''\!\left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right)
+ \frac{2h}{|\vec{x}|}\,
f'\!\left(\frac{|\vec{x}|}{h}\right)
\right],
\end{align}
