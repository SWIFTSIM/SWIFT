The quantum potential or quantum pressure $Q$ writes:

\begin{equation}
Q = \frac{\hbar^2}{2\,m^2_{\chi}} \frac{\nabla^2 \sqrt{\rho}}{\sqrt{\rho}}
\end{equation}

The corresponding acceleration is thus simply the gradient of the potential/pressure and writes (must be checked):

\begin{equation}
  \frac{\rm{d}}{\rm{d}t} \vec{v} = \vec{\nabla} Q
                       = \frac{\hbar^2}{2\,m^2_{\chi}}  \vec{\nabla} \left(  \frac{\nabla^2 \sqrt{\rho}}{\sqrt{\rho}} \right)
\end{equation}
