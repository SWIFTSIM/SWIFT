The quantum potential $Q$ writes:

\begin{eqnarray}
  Q & = & \frac{\hbar^2}{2\,m^2_{\chi}} \frac{\nabla^2 \sqrt{\rho}}{\sqrt{\rho}}\\
    & = & \frac{\hbar^2}{2\,m^2_{\chi}} \left(  \frac{\nabla^2\rho}{2\rho} -  \frac{|\nabla\rho|^2}{4\rho^2} \right)
\end{eqnarray}

The corresponding acceleration is thus simply the gradient of the potential and writes:

\begin{equation}
  \frac{\rm{d}}{\rm{d}t} \vec{v} = \vec{\nabla} Q                       
\end{equation}


The quantum pressure $P$ can be defined by:

\begin{equation}
  \frac{\vec{\nabla} P}{\rho} =  \vec{\nabla} Q                     
\end{equation}
