\section{Context and goal}

Branch \code{darwin/gear\_radiation} implements a Fire-2-style
\citep{Hopkins2018} subgrid HII photoionization model for the GEAR
feedback scheme: each active star sorts nearby gas particles by
distance and spends a photon-rate budget on them, closest first, until
the budget is exhausted, tagging the affordable particles as
``ionized'' and holding their temperature at a floor value until the
next rebuild. The model is validated against two published, closely
related reference algorithms:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \citet{Hu2017}, Appendix A: the four-source D-type expansion
    convergence test that our own ``spherical'' (\code{nside=0})
    mode reproduces directly;
  \item \citet{Smith2021}, Section 3.3.3--3.3.4 and Figs.~1--2: the
    same D-type test, plus an angular (HealPix) budget-splitting
    scheme designed to avoid ``mass-biasing'' -- a dense clump near a
    source absorbing a disproportionate share of the photon budget
    regardless of the small solid angle it subtends.

  Both papers is the ground truth this branch is being validated
  against; the example \code{examples/SubgridTests/\allowbreak
  StromgrenSphereClumpSmith2021/} reproduces their exact test
  geometry (four 19.2\,M$_\odot$ co-located sources, $n=100\,\rm
  cm^{-3}$ background, a $10^4\,\rm cm^{-3}$ clump 20\,pc away).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Where things stand (2026-07-16)}

The angular-splitting feature (Phase 1--3 of the original
implementation plan) and the \code{space\_regrid} $h_{\rm hii}$-awareness fix
were completed and validated in earlier sessions on this branch. This
logbook picks up from there: Darwin reported that the example produced
essentially no HII region growth at all, which led to finding and
fixing a real units bug (Section~\ref{sec:bugs}), followed by an
ongoing validation campaign against the two papers
(Section~\ref{sec:validation}) that is still in progress
(Section~\ref{sec:open}).

\subsection{How to keep this logbook current}

Each numbered file covers one topic and should stay short enough to
read and edit in one sitting:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \code{01\_algorithm.tex} -- the algorithm and its formulas,
    update only when the code's physics actually changes;
  \item \code{02\_task\_graph.tex} -- how the search is wired into
    the task graph (task types, unlocking, activation, locking,
    run-time recursion); update when the task-graph implementation
    itself changes, not for validation results;
  \item \code{03\_bugs\_and\_fixes.tex} -- a dated log of bugs found
    and fixed, append-only (do not delete old entries once fixed, they
    are the record of what was wrong and why);
  \item \code{04\_validation.tex} -- the running comparison against
    Hu/Smith: tables, figures, and their interpretation. Replace
    superseded numbers rather than accumulating them once a fix makes
    old numbers wrong (unlike 03, this section describes the current
    best understanding, not a history);
  \item \code{05\_open\_items.tex} -- what remains undecided or
    undone. Move an item out once it's resolved, into either 03 or 04
    as appropriate.
\end{itemize}
Figures live in \code{Figures/}, named
\code{<config>\_<slice-or-projection>.png}.
